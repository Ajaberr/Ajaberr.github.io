DivHacks Winner

My team and I achieved 3rd place at Columbia DivHacks 2024 and earned $1000 for winning the Best Financial Hack Challenge sponsored by Capital One. At Columbia DivHacks 2024, my team and I built PennyPals, an investment game promoting financial literacy for children (especially underrepresented groups). The app teaches smart financial habits, risk evaluation, and entrepreneurial thinking through digestible educational content, stock market simulations, and collaborative parent-child interaction. Parents simulate deposits while children make investment decisions, fostering open discussions about finances and enhancing money management across generations. While it's a game, PennyPals can also function as a custodial money management app, allowing for real investments as children get older[Github].

Skills: HTML, CSS, EJS

PlantCare

PlantCare is a long term project I have been working on. It aims to automate the process of taking care of plants. It does this by automatically providing the soil with water, nutrients, and also ensures optimum pH. For water and pH, I used arduino code and their respective sensors to respond to abnormal soil humidty and pH. I also am in the process of creating an ML algorithm that uses quantitative soil data to detect the amount and time when a certain type of nutrient should be added to the soil. As a part of this, I have trained a Deep learning model of accuracy 95% using pure python, numpy, and matplotlib in order to detect 4 different levels of nitrogen deficiency in rice plants using image datasets[Github].

Skills: Python, Advanced Mathematics, Machine Learning Algorithms, Matplotlib

Research

Received the maximum Scholarship in the Polygence Research Program, mentored by a Stanford Graduate.
Paper Abstract:
This paper investigates the linguistic characteristics of GPT-4, focusing on how it emulates statistical human vocabulary traits and biases towards specific words. It examines GPT-4's ability to adapt its writing style based on given prompts. The study compares GPT-4's outputs in Shakespearean and Dickensian styles with authentic texts from these authors, assessing vocabulary traits and their statistical patterns. By using Zipf's law, the research identifies and analyzes patterns in GPT-4's language generation, contributing to a deeper understanding of its capabilities in natural language processing. The study utilizes datasets from Kaggle [2], CopyLeaks AI [3], and Chatbot APIs.

Skills: Python, Statistics, Matplotlib, NLP
